Tacos Al Pastor

What are Tacos Al Pastor?

Al Pasto is seasoned and marinated pork, thinly sliced pieces are cut to make for the best tacos. This traditonal Mexican dish originated in the Central Mexican region of Puebla and Mexico City. This delicious dish is roasted to be served with sliced pieces of pineapple, onion, cilantro and your ideal red or green salsa on top of the cooked tortilla.

Ingredients

  • Boneless pork shoulder
  • 3 dried Guajillo chilies
  • 2 chili's de arbol
  • 1 ancho chili
  • 2 tbsp achiote paste
  • 1 white onion
  • 3/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp Mexican oregano
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/8 tsp ground clove
  • corn tortillas
  • 1 white onion diced
  • 1 bunch cilantro chopped
  • limes quartered
  • hot sauce
  • salsa verde or roja

Instructions

Making the Chile Blend

  1. Bring a pot of water and add the chili's (ancho, guaillo, and de arbol). Boil chili's for 15 mins or until water is boiled.

Making the Marinade

  1. To a blender add the boied chili's, achiote paste, onion, pineapple juice vinegar, garlic, salt, Mexican oregano, cinnamon, clove. Blend until pureed.

Preparing the pork

  1. Trim the fat from the pork shoulder and slice meat into 1/4 inch slices.
  2. Place pork in a plastic bag that seals or container to pour the chile mixture over the pork.
  3. Using gloves mix the chile marinade and pork then put in the refregiator for 4 hours or overnight. Allow meat to come to room temp before cooking.
  4. Preheat oven to 250°F. Remove all racks inside except the bottom one.
  5. In a cast iron skillet or baking tray place the cut pineapple bottoms. Insert a wooden or metal skewer or long wooden chopstick in the center of the pineapple.
  6. Start layering the marinated pork in the skewer. Cap the end of the skewer with a trimmed pineapple.
  7. Slow roast in oven at 250°F for 2 1/2 hours or until meat is cooked.
  8. In the last hour pour the pan juices to give it a caramelized and crispy look.
  9. Cut with knife in downward motion

Ready to serve

  1. Serve in small tortillas topped with chopped onion, cilantro, squeeze lime juice and add your favorite green or red salsa.
  2. Serve and Enjoy!
